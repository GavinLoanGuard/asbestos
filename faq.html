Costs & Pricing

Asbestos testing in Calgary typically costs $30-$60 per sample for laboratory analysis. Multi-sample packages (3-5 samples) range from $100-$200, and full home inspections cost $300-$600.

On-site sample collection by a technician adds to the cost compared to drop-off service. Rush results are also available for an additional fee. Contact us for an exact quote based on your specific needs.

Price variations reflect differences in service levels. Lower prices typically mean drop-off only service, longer turnaround times, and basic reporting. Higher prices often include on-site collection, faster results, and detailed consultation.

When comparing quotes, make sure you understand what's included in each price.

Testing Process

Sample collection typically takes 30 minutes to 2 hours depending on the number of materials being tested. Laboratory results are usually available within 24-48 hours for standard service.

Rush or same-day results are available for an additional fee if you need answers quickly for a time-sensitive project.

While DIY asbestos test kits exist, professional sampling is strongly recommended. Improper sample collection can release dangerous fibers into your home and may produce inaccurate results.

Certified professionals use proper safety equipment, containment procedures, and sampling techniques to protect you and your family while ensuring accurate test results.

The testing process includes:

  • Initial consultation to identify materials of concern
  • Safe sample collection by a trained technician
  • Laboratory analysis using approved methods (PLM or TEM)
  • Delivery of certified results and recommendations

Regulations & Requirements

Yes. Under Alberta's Occupational Health and Safety Code, testing is required before disturbing materials that may contain asbestos in buildings constructed before 1990.

This requirement protects both workers and building occupants from asbestos exposure. Many Calgary contractors will not begin work on older homes without seeing asbestos test results first.

While the legal requirements under Alberta's OH&S Code specifically apply to work situations, testing is strongly recommended for DIY renovations to protect your own health and that of your family.

Asbestos fibers released during renovation can remain airborne for hours and spread throughout your home, potentially affecting everyone living there.

Results & Next Steps

If asbestos is detected, you'll receive guidance on next steps based on your situation. Undisturbed asbestos in good condition can often be safely left in place and monitored.

If removal is necessary for renovation or due to deterioration, it must be performed by licensed abatement contractors using proper containment and safety procedures. We can connect you with qualified abatement professionals in Calgary.

Asbestos-containing materials that are in good condition and left undisturbed generally pose minimal risk. Asbestos becomes dangerous when fibers are released into the air.

This happens when materials are cut, drilled, sanded, scraped, or deteriorate over time. Intact materials in good condition can often be safely managed in place with periodic monitoring.

Specific Materials

You cannot tell by looking—only laboratory testing can confirm whether popcorn ceilings contain asbestos. However, textured ceilings installed before 1990 frequently do contain asbestos.

If your Calgary home was built or had ceiling work done before 1990, testing is recommended before any work that would disturb the texture.

Not all vermiculite insulation contains asbestos, but a significant amount does. Much of the vermiculite sold in North America came from the Libby, Montana mine, which was contaminated with tremolite asbestos.

Because you cannot tell contaminated vermiculite from safe vermiculite by appearance alone, testing is recommended for all vermiculite insulation before disturbance.

Vinyl floor tiles and the backing material (mastic) from before 1990 commonly contain asbestos. The 9"x9" tile size is particularly associated with asbestos content, though other sizes can also contain it.

Testing is recommended before removing old floor tiles or scraping adhesive from subflooring.
