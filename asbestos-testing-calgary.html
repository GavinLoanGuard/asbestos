Asbestos Testing in Calgary

Professional asbestos testing services for Calgary homes and businesses. We connect you with certified technicians and accredited laboratories to identify asbestos-containing materials safely and accurately.

Why Asbestos Testing Matters in Calgary

Calgary has a significant stock of homes and commercial buildings constructed during the decades when asbestos was commonly used in building materials. Properties built before 1990 are particularly likely to contain asbestos in various forms, from insulation and flooring to ceiling textures and pipe wrapping.

Asbestos becomes dangerous when its microscopic fibers become airborne. This typically happens when asbestos-containing materials are disturbed during renovations, repairs, or demolition. Without proper testing, you cannot visually identify whether a material contains asbestos—only laboratory analysis can confirm its presence.

When You Should Consider Testing

  • Before any renovation or demolition project in a pre-1990 building
  • When buying or selling an older home
  • If you notice damaged, crumbling, or deteriorating building materials
  • When required by your contractor, insurer, or lender
  • For peace of mind in any older Calgary property

Common Materials Tested for Asbestos

Asbestos was valued for its heat resistance, strength, and insulating properties. As a result, it found its way into dozens of building products. In Calgary homes, testing is commonly requested for:

Ceiling tiles & textures
Vermiculite insulation
Vinyl floor tiles
Pipe & duct insulation
Drywall & joint compound
Cement siding & roofing

The Asbestos Testing Process

Understanding what to expect helps Calgary property owners feel confident about the testing process. Here's how it typically works:

1

Initial Consultation

You describe your property, its age, and which materials you're concerned about. We match you with a qualified testing professional in Calgary.

2

On-Site Sample Collection

A certified technician visits your property to safely collect small samples of suspected materials using proper containment procedures.

3

Laboratory Analysis

Samples are analyzed at an accredited Alberta laboratory using polarized light microscopy (PLM) or transmission electron microscopy (TEM).

4

Results & Recommendations

You receive a detailed lab report identifying whether asbestos is present. If positive, guidance on safe next steps is provided.

Types of Asbestos Testing Available

Bulk Sample Testing involves collecting small physical samples of building materials suspected to contain asbestos. This is the most common form of testing and is typically used before renovations.

Air Quality Testing measures the concentration of asbestos fibers in the air. This testing may be appropriate after disturbance of suspected materials or during abatement projects.

Full Property Assessments provide a comprehensive survey of an entire building to identify all potential asbestos-containing materials. This is often requested during real estate transactions.
