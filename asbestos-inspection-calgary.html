Asbestos Inspection in Calgary

Comprehensive asbestos surveys for Calgary properties. Our inspection service identifies all potential asbestos-containing materials throughout your home or building, with detailed documentation and certified lab testing.

What is an Asbestos Inspection?

An asbestos inspection is a comprehensive survey of a property to identify all materials that may contain asbestos. Unlike testing specific materials, a full inspection systematically evaluates the entire building to create a complete picture of potential asbestos hazards.

During an inspection, a trained professional examines accessible areas of your Calgary property, documents suspected asbestos-containing materials, assesses their condition, and collects samples for laboratory analysis. You receive a detailed report that can guide renovation decisions, satisfy due diligence requirements, or provide peace of mind about your property.

Who Needs an Asbestos Inspection?

An Inspection is Recommended For:

  • Home Buyers: Know what you're purchasing before closing on an older Calgary home
  • Home Sellers: Disclose property conditions and avoid surprises during the sale
  • Property Managers: Meet regulatory requirements for commercial buildings
  • Major Renovators: Plan comprehensive projects with complete information
  • Demolition Projects: Required assessment before building teardown
  • Concerned Homeowners: Peace of mind about your family's safety

What the Inspection Covers

A comprehensive asbestos inspection examines all accessible areas of your property where asbestos-containing materials might be present:

Interior Spaces

Ceiling textures, wall materials, flooring, window putty, and insulation around pipes and ducts.

Attic Areas

Vermiculite and other insulation types, ductwork insulation, and any exposed building materials.

Basement & Mechanical

Furnace insulation, pipe wrapping, floor tiles, ceiling tiles, and ductwork materials.

Exterior Materials

Siding, roofing materials, soffits, and exterior cement products where accessible.

The Inspection Process

Visual Assessment: The inspector systematically examines your property, identifying materials that are likely to contain asbestos based on age, appearance, and application method.

Documentation: Each suspected material is documented with its location, condition, and accessibility. Photos may be taken to support the report.

Sample Collection: Small samples are collected from each unique material type for laboratory analysis. Proper safety procedures are followed to prevent fiber release.

Laboratory Testing: Samples are analyzed at an accredited Alberta laboratory to confirm which materials contain asbestos.

Detailed Report: You receive a comprehensive report identifying all tested materials, their asbestos content, condition assessment, and recommendations for management or removal if needed.

Inspection vs. Targeted Testing

A full asbestos inspection differs from targeted testing in scope and purpose. Targeted testing is appropriate when you need to know about specific materials before a defined project. A full inspection provides a complete inventory of asbestos-containing materials throughout the property.

For real estate transactions or comprehensive renovation planning, a full inspection is typically more appropriate. For smaller projects affecting specific materials, targeted testing may be sufficient and more cost-effective.

Inspection Costs

Full property inspections in Calgary typically range from $300-$600 for residential properties, depending on size and complexity. This includes the on-site survey, sample collection, laboratory analysis of multiple samples, and a detailed report. Commercial properties are quoted individually based on square footage and building complexity.
